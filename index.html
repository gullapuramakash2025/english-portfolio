Listening Activity

Listening and summarising

Listening

Listening Activity – Podcast on Meaningfulness in Life

As part of the listening activity in the Technical English Communication course, we listened to a podcast discussing the concept of meaningfulness in life. The podcast explored how individuals often search for meaning through relationships, achievements, personal values, and contributions to society.

One of the key takeaways from the podcast was that meaningfulness does not always come from major accomplishments but often from everyday actions and connections. Small acts such as helping others, maintaining strong relationships, and pursuing personal passions can contribute significantly to a meaningful life.

After listening to the podcast, students were asked to summarise the key ideas and reflect on what meaningfulness means in their own lives.

Speaking Activity

Group discussion

Speaking

Group Discussion – “5 Minute Delivery: Boon or Bane?”

Students participated in a group discussion on the topic “5 Minute Delivery: Boon or Bane?”. The discussion explored the growing trend of ultra-fast delivery services provided by online platforms.

During the discussion, several positive aspects were highlighted, including convenience, time saving, and improved access to essential goods. However, concerns were also raised about the pressure it places on delivery workers, environmental impact, and the sustainability of such business models.

The discussion helped students practice expressing opinions, listening to others' viewpoints, and structuring arguments in a clear and respectful manner.

Reading Activity

Book review

Reading

Book Review – “A Piece of String” by Guy de Maupassant

“A Piece of String” is a short story by Guy de Maupassant that focuses on themes of reputation, suspicion, and human pride.

The story follows Monsieur Hauchecorne, a man who is falsely accused of stealing a lost wallet after he is seen picking up a piece of string from the ground. Despite repeatedly trying to explain his innocence, the villagers refuse to believe him. The accusation damages his reputation and continues to haunt him.

The story highlights how easily misunderstandings and rumors can affect a person's life and how society often judges individuals without proper evidence. It also reflects on human stubbornness and the desire to defend one’s honor.

The story leaves a strong impression about the importance of truth, perception, and social judgment.

Reflection

Course reflection

The Technical English Communication course has helped develop several important communication skills. Through activities such as listening exercises, group discussions, and reading analysis, students were encouraged to actively engage with language rather than simply study it theoretically.

The listening activity improved the ability to identify key ideas and summarise information effectively. The group discussion activity helped build confidence in expressing opinions and participating in structured conversations.

Reading and reviewing literature such as “A Piece of String” encouraged deeper thinking about themes, interpretation, and critical analysis.

Overall, the course has contributed to improving listening, speaking, and analytical skills, which are essential for both academic success and professional communication.
