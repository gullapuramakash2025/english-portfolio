About

Background & mindset

Akash is a first-year B.Tech student at Vellore Institute of Technology (VIT), Vellore, interested in technology, entrepreneurship, and building digital products.

He chose B.Tech because it acts as the bridge between his interests growing up and his long-term goal of pursuing digital entrepreneurship. While reviewing the curriculum, he noticed courses like Data Structures that specifically develop structured problem solving — a skill that is highly valued in the industry he hopes to enter.

He views college as a place to develop independent thinking and practical skills rather than just passively receiving information in lectures. Instead of only being taught information, he wants to learn how to educate himself, challenge assumptions, and apply theoretical concepts to real-world projects.

College expectations vs reality

Before joining college, Akash imagined instant "movie-style" friend groups. In reality, he discovered that relationships tend to form more gradually. Many of the strongest friendships grow through shared academic challenges, late-night study sessions, and collaborative problem solving.

This gradual pace has made him appreciate depth over speed — investing in consistent effort, honest conversations, and shared experiences over time.

Academic Activities

Learning experiences

Reflection

Podcast Reflection

Akash participated in a classroom activity where students listened to and summarized a podcast discussing meaningfulness in life and the idea that purpose often comes from consistent growth and personal development.

The activity reinforced the importance of long-term habits over instant results, echoing his own belief that small, repeated efforts build the foundation for future goals.

Laboratory

Chemistry Laboratory Work

During chemistry lab sessions, Akash performed titration experiments including acid–base titration and redox titration. These experiments required precise measurements, careful observation, and calculations to determine unknown concentrations.

This hands-on work trained his attention to detail and discipline — the same qualities that transfer well to debugging code and validating results in programming.

Coursework

Data Structures Learning

Through coursework in data structures, Akash has begun developing algorithmic thinking and problem solving skills using C and C++. The course focuses on organizing data efficiently and designing algorithms to solve computational problems.

These concepts form the backbone of his future goals in full stack development and digital product building.

Running

Discipline

For a long time, my asthma felt like an obstacle I couldn't get past, especially since no medication seemed to provide a long-term fix. My father always believed that exercise was the key to managing it, which is why I started running back in the third grade. It took a while to really find my stride, but two years ago, I finally committed to it. I set a goal to hit a 20-minute 5K, and through that consistency, I managed to bring my time down to 22:37. It felt good to see that progress and realize that my lungs could actually handle the work. Since starting college, that routine has completely dropped off, and I haven't been out for a run at all. But looking back at that 22:37 reminds me that I’ve already done the hard work of building a foundation. The goal now isn't to do something impossible; it's just to get back to the consistency that I know actually works for me.
