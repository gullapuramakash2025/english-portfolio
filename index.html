First-Year Transition

Settling into college life

Semester One

From School to Campus – Adjusting to a New Life

Moving from school to college meant getting used to a completely new environment — a different city, hostel life, and a packed timetable. The first few weeks were about finding my way around campus, managing classes on my own, and learning how to balance studies with everything else happening around me.

I realised that college is less about memorising content and more about understanding concepts and applying them. Simple things like asking doubts in class, forming study groups, and organising my notes made a big difference in how confident I felt.

This phase taught me that growth happens gradually — through everyday routines, small improvements, and being open to learning from people around me.

Campus Life & Activities

People, clubs, and conversations

Campus Life

Clubs, Hostels & Late-Night Conversations

One of the most important parts of my college journey so far has been the people I have met — classmates, seniors, and friends from different branches and backgrounds.

Through hostel life, club meetings, and random late-night conversations, I have learnt how valuable it is to listen to different perspectives. Discussing career plans, college experiences, technology, hobbies, and even small everyday problems has helped me become more open-minded and expressive.

These interactions have made me more comfortable speaking in groups, sharing my thoughts, and accepting feedback. They are a big part of how I am growing as a person, not just as a student.

Projects & Learning

Building skills step by step

Academics

Early Projects and Academic Milestones

My academic journey so far has focused on building a strong base in core subjects like programming, mathematics, and problem solving. Each course has added a different layer to how I think about technology and real-world applications.

Alongside coursework, I have started working on small projects and practice problems — from basic programs to exploring new concepts beyond the syllabus. These projects may be simple, but they are the first steps towards building confidence as a developer.

Each assignment, lab, and project has taught me something — not just content-wise, but also about time management, debugging patiently, and learning from mistakes.

Over time, I want to explore more projects related to my interests in computer science while keeping my fundamentals strong.

Reflection & Future Plans

Looking back, thinking ahead

When I look back at my college journey so far, I see a mix of small wins, doubts, new habits, and gradual progress. Adjusting to a new environment, meeting new people, and handling academic pressure have all pushed me out of my comfort zone.

I have learned that consistency matters more than sudden bursts of effort — whether it is in studying, practising guitar, playing sports, or working on myself. Moments of failure or confusion have also taught me to be more patient and resilient.

Going forward, I want to deepen my technical skills, participate in more projects and events, and continue building meaningful relationships on campus. My goal is to leave college not just with a degree, but with clarity about who I am and what I want to work towards.
